<h1>Our Objectives</h1> <h3>1. Connect development teams across government</h3> <p>The primary aim of the group is to connect teams through a series of mechanisms:-</p> <ul> <li><strong>Lean Coffee</strong></li> <li><strong>Facilitated Events</strong></li> <li><strong>Mailing List</strong></li> <li><strong>X-Gov Slack</strong></li> </ul> <h3>2. Learning and development</h3> <p>One aim of the group is to understand and faciliate Learning and development within teams and understand and share what are we doing to progress our careers.</p> <p>Addressing questions and sharing information such as</p> <p><strong>What Learning resources are being used ?</strong></p> <p>e.g. we know</p> <p>Several teams have Pluralsight accounts</p> <p>O'Reilly Online Learning is also a popular subscription</p> <p><strong>What Learning techniques are adopted ?</strong></p> <p>e.g.</p> <ul> <li>Learning techniques</li> <li>Pair programming</li> <li>Hack days</li> <li>Code retreats</li> </ul> <p><strong>How are Apprenticeships used ?</strong></p> <p>e.g. Some departments have used an <a href="https://digitalpeople.blog.gov.uk/2020/02/03/how-do-you-attract-more-software-developers-into-your-department/">apprenticeship scheme</a></p> <p><strong>How are we Finding time for learning ?</strong></p> <p>Finding enough time to keep skills up to date is an issue. Developers are sometimes allocated a percentage of time from their working week for self-learning. Others are too busy and learn during evenings and weekends.</p> <h3>3. Championing standards</h3> <p>An aim of the group is to champion x-government standards to do this we aim to understand</p> <p><strong>What standards are in place within government software engineering communities?</strong></p> <p>For</p> <ul> <li>Technology choices</li> <li>Techniques</li> </ul> <p><strong>What Developer standards are there ?</strong></p> <p>Like <a href="https://github.com/DEFRA/software-development-standards">Defra's developer standards</a></p> <p><strong>How are standards communicated within departments?</strong></p> <h3>4. Promoting diversity and inclusion</h3> <p>An aim of the group is to understand how can we improve diversity and inclusion within our government software engineering community? and promote greater diversity and understand :-</p> <p><strong>What are departments already doing?</strong></p> <p>An aim of the group is to understand what departments and bodies are doing in this area.</p> <p><strong>Sharing recruitment experience</strong></p> <p>An aim of the group is to understand and share recruitment experience.</p> <h3>5. Represent the software engineering community in DDAT conversations</h3> <p>An aim of the group is to discuss and represent the community in relation to the [DDAT Framework] (https://www.gov.uk/guidance/software-developer)</p>
